## What is a Second Brain?


In his book, [Building a Second Brain: A Proven Method to Organize Your Digital Life and Unlock Your Creative Potential](https://www.goodreads.com/en/book/show/59616977), Tiago Forte shares a method of personal knowledge management and productivity that involves creating a comprehensive, searchable database of one's ideas, notes, and reference materials. This system aims to externalize one's thinking and build a "second brain" to store and recall information, reduce forgetfulness, and increase efficiency. This system may include digital tools such as note-taking apps, task managers, knowledge management software, and physical notebooks and binders. Tiago believes that the human brain has a limited capacity to store information. By externalizing this information, one can offload some of this burden and use the brain for higher-level thinking and problem solving.


## PARA System


Tiago Forte's second brain course and the system uses a framework called PARA, which stands for Projects, Areas, Resources, and Archives. PARA is a hierarchical organization system that provides a structure for categorizing and storing information in a second brain.

- **Projects:** The first level of the PARA framework is Projects, defined as long-term, outcome-oriented goals or initiatives.
- **Areas:** The second level of PARA is Areas, which are the broader categories or topics that a person wants to keep track of, such as work, personal life, or hobbies.
- **Resources:** The third level of PARA is Resources, which are the specific pieces of information, such as notes, articles, and documents, that support a particular project or area.
- **Archives**: Finally, the fourth level of PARA is Archives, which are the completed projects and finished resources stored for reference and historical purposes.

Most of the Second Brain templates use the PARA framework to help you categorize and organize your information in a way that makes it easy to find, recall, and use, thus aiding in constructing an effective second brain system.
