Journaling has long been a popular way to reflect on thoughts, emotions, and experiences. It is a therapeutic activity and can help improve mental health, increase self-awareness, and even boost productivity.


Enter Notion and Notion journal templates. For those unfamiliar with Notion, it is a powerful organization and productivity tool that allows users to create custom databases and to-do lists, take notes, write, and more. One of its most powerful features is the ability to use templates created by Notion experts, which are pre-designed pages or databases that jumpstart your next project or task. This blog post will focus on templates that make it easy for you to write and track your daily journal entries in Notion.


You can also browse our favorite [Notion templates](/86f5352f0a834816a04e6d9d5a00be48), [courses](/828553e628164e73be1b8ee88d3671ba), and [tools](/917f77cb548e442ab5ff4e1a60f6176b).

Overall, Notion can be a safe and secure option for journaling if you take the appropriate steps to protect your privacy and security. As with any online tool, it's always a good idea to be aware of potential risks and to protect your personal information.


### Is Notion free to use?

For users who need additional storage space or more advanced features, such as the ability to use custom domains or access more block embeds, Notion also offers paid plans. These plans start at $4 per month for individuals and $8 per month for teams.
